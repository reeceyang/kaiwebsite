thinking forward thoughts

December 15, 2022

in my old room in tEp (my living community, a co-ed frat across the river from MIT), there was a ladder chained at its top end to the ceiling and pivoted at its bottom end on the floor. it reached up from the floorboards to a sofa hanging from the ceiling. there was slack in the chains, so the ladder would swing back and forth if you grabbed it and rattled it. you could climb this ladder to get onto the sofa if you were careful and leaned your weight forward while climbing so that the ladder, too, leaned forward: at tEp we called this procedure “thinking forward thoughts”.

at some point in the semester, i went off my medication for a couple of weeks because, for one reason or another, i was continually unable to get a refill. when i finally began taking my meds regularly again, i was horrendously behind on work and had fallen into the habit of sleeping upwards of 14 hours a day. from this point until the end of the semester, although i was doing less work than ever, i entertained the thought of dropping out of MIT almost daily.

even now, after my final projects have all passed and i’m feeling like myself again, i can’t articulate what makes me feel happy and what doesn’t, and in what amounts. i do know that i sorely miss high school, the feeling of hard work and of learning whatever i wanted whenever i wanted—i felt fulfilled back then even if i didn’t always feel “happy”; i felt good about my life in a larger sense. since the beginning of covid, this feeling has eluded me. i feel like i’m forever chasing a past version of myself. i keep trying to reflect and discover what went wrong: what was it about the past that i’m missing now? what can i do differently to unearth some feeling of fulfillment again?

notably, this mindset has not helped me much. it’s impossible to directly translate my high school experiences onto a whole new setting and, in some ways, a whole new person. in high school i cared about doing competitions, seeing them as a way to prepare myself for my future career, the details of which were not particularly important. in college i care about actually accomplishing things with the resources i have now. in high school i did not have people to care unconditionally about me, or at least i was not in a place to truly appreciate them; now, i belong to a community, i love my partner dearly, i put more effort into my relationship with my family.

so let’s take a break from reflecting on the past. here are some things i want to keep doing, going forward:

  • i mentored two first-years in physics this semester, and this was one of the most rewarding experiences i’ve had at MIT thus far. i became much more confident in my understanding of physics in general; i met people that i probably never would have interacted with otherwise; i learned a lot about teaching; most importantly, i am so glad that i was able to help other people in the way i wish i could have been helped in the past, learning physics on my own without anyone to ask for guidance. i had my last meeting with one of my mentees this week, and she had made me a bracelet to show her appreciation for my mentorship this semester, which nearly made me cry. so i definitely want to continue with this program in the future.

  • moving to tEp was one of the best choices i made in the past year; next year, i’ll be more involved, as rush chair. i’ll be organizing our spring rush, CPW events, and fall rush. i’m a bit apprehensive about the workload, but i also am excited about contributing to a community i love in this way.

  • i discovered this year that doing art is extremely relaxing and restorative for me, and actually works as a little break between doing assignments. i made some stickers (pictured below) for a fundraiser, and i’m really proud of them. i want to continue intentionally putting time into art in the future; in particular, i might take CMS.306 (Making Comics and Sequential Art) in the spring!

  • my main struggle in 18.701 this semester was not being able to keep up with the material at the pace it was being taught; i found the lectures insufficient to gain a good understanding of the material. every week i would go to lecture (and be very confused) → attempt the pset → scour through the textbook for explanation → realize while watching the lecture later that things made a lot more sense after doing all these steps. towards the end of the semester, i tried to rigorously read the relevant textbook chapter on my own before watching a lecture or doing the pset, and this made things way easier. go figure! i will definitely do this more in the future.

  • i started exercising regularly near the end of the semester—swimming and doing workouts with my partner. this was very enjoyable and did not take nearly the amount of time i thought it would; it was actually very easy to slip a 20-minute workout into my day. i really want to become more fit, so i’ll try my best to make this into a stronger routine.
