summer updates

June 15, 2022

We’re a month into summer now, which feels crazy to say. I’ve settled into a more stable routine, and I’m really enjoying myself. Here are some of the things I’ve been up to!

I met with my UROP PI this morning for an hour of discussion about the research I’m starting to do. I don’t fully understand it yet, to be honest. But neither does my PI, and I find my work now a lot more engaging and challenging than was the project I worked on during the school year. I’m leaning into being a more active part of the lab – attending group meetings, raising dumb ideas during talks with my mentors. I still feel nervous sometimes, but at least I no longer worry that they’ll fire me.

I’m also getting better at working eight hours a day. But this has left a little less room for watching movies with my partner, or opening up my sketchbook, or exercising. Nevertheless, I find some time: I went paddleboarding at the Mystic Lakes last week; hiked up Mt. Eisenhower, bruising my feet and bloodying my toes, this past weekend; and will go camping at Acadia National Park, in Maine, this weekend. Every night, my partner and I take a walk along the esplanade; sometimes we buy boba, too.

There’s an online introductory plasma science and fusion course going on for the next two weeks hosted by the Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory. It runs from 12pm to 5pm each day with some breaks, and so far it’s been really cool, listening to professors from all over give talks about their respective fields. Some topics that have been covered so far: stellarators, inertial confinement fusion, tokamaks, plasma waves. Tomorrow’s selection of talks discusses magnetic reconnection, heliosphere plasmas, and van Allen radiation belts. I love learning about this stuff – both because it’s cool, and because it’s nice to expand outside my little subfield of plasma physics. Also, it’s great to learn with no expectation of being tested. I just sit and absorb the information.

I’m working as a part-time tour guide for MIT Admissions this summer. My first practice tour was this Monday, and I spent the entire night before worrying about it. Despite that, and despite some blunders during the tour, it went well overall. During the summer, tours are much larger and people are much more lively than during the spring tours that I shadowed. This put me at ease pretty quickly, and most of the tour was quite fun!

I signed up for another tour tomorrow morning, and another next Tuesday morning. Tour guiding pays really well: $16/hour, and you log 1.5 hours in your payroll for each hour that you actually work. I’m hoping that I’ll become more comfortable with it as time goes on; right now, I’m still unreasonably nervous about tomorrow.

I really enjoy everything that I’m doing right now, and I’m stoked for the next three (!!) months of summer. I’m spending one of those months back at home in California, but I’m sure I’ll still be able to do good work there. I’m a little apprehensive about being back for one whole month…at the same time, I do look forward to seeing my family again. We’ll see how it goes!
