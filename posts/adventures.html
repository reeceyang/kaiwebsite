adventures

January 15, 2024

It seems like I’ll be staying in Boston this coming summer. I’m not yet sure what I’ll do here—there are a few research projects I could continue, or I could seek out something new. But the location seems fairly set, because I need to take care of my turtles and my tortoise, and because I’m not applying to any external research programs.

Staying in a city I’m relatively familiar with, doing work at the same place I go to school all other months of the year, makes me feel a little apprehensive after my adventure-filled summer of 2023, living and working in New Mexico. I thought: could I not also seek out adventures in Boston? But I think there’s something different, not only about being forced to navigate a new place with new people, but also the inherent discomfort that the desert forces upon you as opposed to the city, a 20-square-mile region manufactured for human comfort and convenience.

Still, there are many ways I can grow while I’m here. This IAP, I’m working on research at MIT’s Laboratory for Nuclear Security and Policy. It’s quite unlike any research I’ve done before—for one thing, I’m in the office every weekday, 9am-5pm, with my mentor in the same room as me. Although I was in the office every day in New Mexico, my mentor worked on a different campus than me, and I only saw him once a week. This significantly hampered my progress at some points.

Now, I can turn my swivel chair around and ask my mentor a question whenever I want. He and my PI are both invested in my success and trusting of my capabilities—I feel like they listen sincerely to the concerns I bring up, even though they know better about almost every aspect of the research than I do. Such an encouraging environment is one that I hope to learn to take full advantage of this winter—and this summer, if I choose to continue the project.

Recently I’ve been thinking more about delving deeper into a subject vs. broadening my horizons. I feel some pressure to figure out as soon as possible some worthwhile undertaking, if not my life trajectory overall. I’ve written before about how I feel this is a slightly fruitless desire, since I’ve not yet been exposed to much of the adult world outside academia; still, seeing peers excel in fields that they came into college knowing they wanted to be a part of stings when my research has varied from plasma physics to ecology to international affairs, yet I’ve still not found something I want to settle down and do.

Of course, this concept of “settling down” is fallible in itself. People and trajectories change over time, and even when they aren’t changing, they don’t necessarily follow one-lane roads. I spent my fall semester using computer vision to count salmon, and though I wouldn’t go to grad school for this, I can easily see a future where I keep doing this for ten hours a week on the side as an easy reprieve from physics—and one that I know is helping the planet.

At this point, though, I have explored a lot and have found some things I’m satisfied with spending the next couple of years on. So I am deciding to apply to the five-year M.S. program that MIT’s department of nuclear science and engineering offers. (Five years as in one additional year on top of the regular four to earn a B.S.) Though I’m not sure what kind of research I’ll do for my thesis, I know now I’m really interested in nuclear engineering in general, and I want to complete a deep project like a master’s thesis that I can sink two whole years into.

  • swam in a bunch of random bodies of water

  • began going to the gym consistently (just got a membership at a nearby climbing gym here in Boston)

  • went on a road trip to Vermont, where I biked 23 miles in the fall foliage

  • ran my first 5k (not a race lol)

  • passed my halfway point in college!!

  • climbed around an abandoned waterpark in Maine

  • went from self-studying machine learning on Kaggle to contributing to a machine learning research project

  • learned C++ lol

  • read Kazuo Ishiguro for the first time, who is now hands down my favorite author
